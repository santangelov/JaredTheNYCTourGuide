NYC Tour FAQs

Planning a New York City tour? Here are the most common questions about booking, timing, custom routes, museum tours (including the Met), and how to get the most out of your time in NYC with Jared.

Booking & scheduling

Jared’s tours are by appointment and built around your timing and interests. The fastest way to start is the Book Your Tour form. Tell us: your preferred date(s), how many people are in your group, and what you’re excited to explore (neighborhoods, history, art, food, architecture).

After you submit, Jared will follow up to shape the best route, pacing, and meeting plan for your group.

Private and custom NYC tours are scheduled based on mutual availability. In practice, that means we can tour whenever works for both of us— mornings, afternoons, or evenings—depending on your itinerary and what you want to see.

Jared offers private and group tours that can focus on neighborhoods, NYC history, architecture, parks, and museums. Many experiences are walking tours, and some can be arranged as bus or coach step‑on tours when that fits your itinerary.

If you want ideas, start on the Destinations page, then we’ll tailor it.

Safety & liability note

While Jared works to make your New York City experience safe, informative, and enjoyable, surprises can happen in any big city. Sidewalks and roads can be uneven, and traffic and pedestrians can be unpredictable. Jared checks in with you during the tour, and is certified in First Aid and CPR.

By booking a tour, you agree to hold Jared blameless for liability. In exchange, you can expect a positive, memorable experience. In over twenty years of touring, liability has not come up for Jared, and it is not an issue he commonly hears about within the NYC tour‑guide community.
